The Philosopher, the Pioneer, the Advocate
RMS, as he's often called, has been one of the most influential figures in the free software movement. His work has helped shape how we think about software freedom and the rights of users to use, modify, and share software. While he is not directly associated with creating Linux (the kernel itself), his vision and tools have been fundamental to the Linux ecosystem. Without the GNU tools, Linux wouldn't be as powerful or usable as it is today.

There shall be no other Free Software Foundation, but there can be other advocates of software freedom.
